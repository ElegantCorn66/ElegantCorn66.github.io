Research

Six projects

My research develops numerical methods that make large electromagnetic, circuit, and quantum-device problems tractable — and that come with an error budget rather than a hope. The projects below are grouped by where the work was done.

On-Chip Electromagnetics Laboratory, Purdue University

Research Assistant · Advisor: Prof. Dan Jiao

Fast solvers

Explicit rank-preserving solver for time-harmonic Maxwell's equations with broadband stability

  • Addresses the low-frequency breakdown of full-wave electromagnetic circuit analysis using a novel explicit rank stabilizer.
  • Runs in O(N) time with minimal additional computational effort.
  • Applied to large multi-scale integrated-circuit problems and to antenna analysis.
Machine learning

Robust physics-informed, data-free machine learning for 3-D electromagnetic analysis

  • An unsupervised physics-informed neural network that solves Maxwell's equations with no training data.
  • Formulates a single objective loss that combines integral-equation and finite-element operators under complicated boundary conditions, so learning stays efficient.
  • Applied to large-scale IC simulation and to full-wave electromagnetic scattering.
Optimization

Error-controlled rank-revealing method for large global optimization problems

  • An error-controlled, rank-revealing optimizer that beats Bayesian optimization, simulated annealing, and genetic algorithms on both runtime and reliability of finding the global optimum.
  • Applied to hard benchmark problems and to real designs: antenna optimization and floor planning for heterogeneously integrated semiconductor systems.
  • Extended to nonlinear optimization problems in machine learning.
Quantum devices

Fast volume-integral-equation modeling of spin-qubit decoherence with non-reciprocal magnetic metasurfaces

  • A rapid solver for decoherence analysis in quantum spin systems.
  • Extensive numerical experiments validate the method on real non-reciprocal anisotropic magnetic metasurfaces used to control decoherence.
Quantum devices

Fast well-conditioned VIE solver for nonlocal optical responses in quantum nanostructures

  • A fast solver for electromagnetic scattering problems arising in quantum device design.
  • Accelerates the volume integral equation while accounting for nonlocal microscopic optical response.
  • Used to simulate the limits that thermal and vacuum fluctuations place on two-spin-qubit gate fidelity.

Helmholtz-Institut Mainz, Mainz University, Germany

Visiting Scholar · Advisor: Prof. Dmitry Budker

Quantum sensing

Reduced contrast of nitrogen-vacancy ensembles in diamond at zero magnetic field

  • Formulated and simulated the dynamics of nuclear spins in nitrogen-vacancy centers, a leading quantum-device platform.
  • Built the experimental platform and its control software, with measurements matching theoretical analysis.

CAS Key Laboratory of Microscale Magnetic Resonance, USTC, China

Research Assistant · Advisor: Prof. Xing Rong

Instrumentation

High-sensitivity quantum magnetometer based on nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond

  • Designed a new schematic for compact, high-sensitivity magnetometry using NV-ensemble diamond.
  • Built a working prototype reaching 500 pT/√Hz — twice the sensitivity of prior work.
