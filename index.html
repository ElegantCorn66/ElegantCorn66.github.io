About

Overview

My work sits at the intersection of computational electromagnetics, numerical linear algebra, and machine learning. The through-line is efficiency with a guarantee: methods that scale to large, multi-scale problems while keeping error under explicit control.

At Purdue's On-Chip Electromagnetics Lab, advised by Prof. Dan Jiao, I developed an explicit rank-preserving solver that removes the low-frequency breakdown in full-wave circuit analysis at O(N) cost, an unsupervised physics-informed neural network that solves Maxwell's equations without any training data, and an error-controlled rank-revealing global optimizer that outperforms Bayesian optimization, simulated annealing, and genetic algorithms on both benchmark and real design problems.

Alongside this, I model quantum devices: spin-qubit decoherence controlled by non-reciprocal magnetic metasurfaces, and nonlocal optical responses in quantum nanostructures. Earlier, at the Helmholtz-Institut Mainz and USTC, I worked experimentally on nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond, including a compact magnetometer reaching 500 pT/√Hz.

I am currently interested in faculty and research positions where these tools can be pushed toward larger systems — heterogeneous integration, quantum device design automation, and learning-based simulation.
